Now, consider wrapping two links inside a div element:

Google YouTube
Facebook

Here, the Google and YouTube links are displayed on one line, while the Facebook link is on another line. This happens because the div tag is a block-level element. The links within the div are grouped into one container, making it easier to apply styles using a class or id.

Now, let's use a span tag for the same two links:

Google YouTube Facebook

Unlike the div tag, the span tag does not force a new line. However, when inspected, we can see that the span acts as a container around the links.
